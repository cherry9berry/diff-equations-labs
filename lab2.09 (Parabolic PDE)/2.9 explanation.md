# Lab 2.9: Parabolic PDE (Heat Equation)

## Work Description
This laboratory work focuses on the analytical and numerical solution of **1D Parabolic Partial Differential Equations**, specifically the **Heat Equation**. The goal is to determine the temperature distribution $u(x,t)$ in a rod over time, given specific initial and boundary conditions.

The solution is performed using the **Method of Separation of Variables (Fourier Method)**, which decomposes the complex PDE into several simpler Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs).

## Mathematical Model
The standard form of the 1D heat equation is:
$$u_t = a^2 u_{xx}$$
where $u(x,t)$ is the temperature, $t$ is time, and $x$ is the spatial coordinate.

### Boundary Conditions
The problems involve various types of boundary conditions (Dirichlet, Neumann, or Robin) at the ends of the segment $[1, 2]$ or $[0, L]$. If the conditions are non-homogeneous, a homogenization step is applied.

### Solution Algorithm (7 Steps)
1.  **Homogenization**: Reducing non-homogeneous boundary conditions to homogeneous ones by introducing an auxiliary function $s(x,t)$.
2.  **Transformation**: Recalculating the equation and initial conditions for the new function $v(x,t)$.
3.  **Eigenvalue Problem**: Solving the Sturm-Liouville problem to find eigenvalues $\lambda_k$ and eigenfunctions $X_k(x)$.
4.  **Fourier Expansion**: Expanding the initial condition $\phi(x)$ and the source term $f(x,t)$ into Fourier series relative to the eigenfunctions.
5.  **Solving Time Components**: Solving the ODE for $T_k(t)$.
6.  **Synthesis**: Constructing the general solution as a sum of the series.
7.  **Final Result**: Combining the auxiliary and transition functions to get $u(x,t)$.
