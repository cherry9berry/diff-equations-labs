# Lab 2.3: Perturbation Method (Asymptotic Base)

## Work Description
This laboratory work covers the basic principles of the **Perturbation Method** (specifically, the Regular Perturbation Method). This method is used to find approximate analytical solutions to equations that contain a small parameter $\epsilon$. 

The task is to find the roots of the algebraic equation:
$$x^3 + \epsilon x - 1 = 0$$
where $\epsilon \ll 1$.

The work involves:
1. Constructing an asymptotic expansion of the solution.
2. Implementing numerical root-finding methods (Bisection, Newton's method).
3. Comparing the accuracy of the $k$-th order asymptotic solutions against numerical "exact" results.

## Mathematical Model
We seek a solution in the form of a power series in $\epsilon$:
$$x(\epsilon) = x_0 + \epsilon x_1 + \epsilon^2 x_2 + \epsilon^3 x_3 + \dots$$

By substituting this series into the original equation and grouping terms by powers of $\epsilon$, we find:
- **Order $\epsilon^0$**: $x_0^3 - 1 = 0 \implies x_0 = 1$.
- **Order $\epsilon^1$**: $3x_0^2 x_1 + x_0 = 0 \implies 3(1)^2 x_1 + 1 = 0 \implies x_1 = -1/3$.
- **Order $\epsilon^2$**: $3x_0 x_1^2 + 3x_0^2 x_2 + x_1 = 0 \implies x_2 = 0$.
- **Order $\epsilon^3$**: $x_1^3 + 6x_0 x_1 x_2 + 3x_0^2 x_3 + x_2 = 0 \implies x_3 = 1/81$.

Final 3rd-order expansion:
$$x(\epsilon) \approx 1 - \frac{\epsilon}{3} + \frac{\epsilon^3}{81}$$

## Implementation & Results
- **Numerical Validation**: Newton's method and Bisection were implemented to find the "true" root.
- **Error Analysis**: The error $|x_{asymp} - x_{exact}|$ was analyzed for various orders of expansion.
- **Log-Log Plot**: The error plot demonstrates that the $k$-th order expansion has an error proportional to $\epsilon^{k+1}$. For very small $\epsilon$ (e.g., $10^{-4}$), even the 1st-order expansion provides extremely high accuracy (order of $10^{-8}$), while the 3rd-order expansion matches the numerical solution within the precision limits of floating-point arithmetic.
