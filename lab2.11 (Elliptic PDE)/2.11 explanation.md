# Lab 2.11: Elliptic PDE (Laplace and Poisson Equations)

## Work Description
This laboratory work focuses on solving **Elliptic Partial Differential Equations**, specifically the **Laplace equation** ($\Delta u = 0$) and the **Poisson equation** ($\Delta u = f(x,y)$). These equations model steady-state processes, such as stationary temperature distribution or electrostatic potential.

The solution is performed using the **Method of Separation of Variables (Fourier Method)** for two common geometries: a rectangle (Cartesian coordinates) and a disk (Polar coordinates).

## Mathematical Model
### 1. Cartesian Coordinates (Rectangle)
Equation: $u_{xx} + u_{yy} = 0$, where $x \in [0, a], y \in [0, b]$.
The **Principle of Superposition** is used if multiple boundary conditions are non-zero. The solution is the sum of four sub-problems (one for each side).

### 2. Polar Coordinates (Disk/Circle)
Equation: $\frac{1}{r} \frac{\partial}{\partial r} (r \frac{\partial u}{\partial r}) + \frac{1}{r^2} \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial \theta^2} = 0$.
Boundary condition: $u(R, \theta) = g(\theta)$.
Solution is sought in the form $u(r, \theta) = R(r)\Phi(\theta)$, leading to the **Poisson Integral Formula** or a Fourier series in $\theta$.

### Solution Algorithm (General)
1.  **Superposition**: Splitting the problem into sub-problems with only one non-homogeneous boundary condition.
2.  **Separation of Variables**: $u = X(x)Y(y)$ or $u = R(r)\Phi(\theta)$.
3.  **Solving ODEs**: Finding eigenfunctions (sines/cosines) and radial/hyperbolic functions.
4.  **Fourier Coefficients**: Calculating expansion coefficients from the given boundary functions.
5.  **Synthesis**: Combining sub-problem solutions into the final $u(x,y)$ or $u(r,\theta)$.
