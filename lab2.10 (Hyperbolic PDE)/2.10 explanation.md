# Lab 2.10: Hyperbolic PDE (Wave Equation)

## Work Description
This laboratory work focuses on the analytical and numerical solution of **1D Hyperbolic Partial Differential Equations**, specifically the **Wave Equation**. The primary goal is to determine the oscillation $u(x,t)$ (e.g., a vibrating string) given specific initial and boundary conditions.

The solution is derived using the **Method of Separation of Variables (Fourier Method)**, which translates the PDE into a system of second-order Ordinary Differential Equations for the time components.

## Mathematical Model
The standard form of the 1D wave equation is:
$$u_{tt} = a^2 u_{xx} + f(x,t)$$
where $u(x,t)$ is the displacement, $t$ is time, and $a$ is the wave propagation speed.

### Initial and Boundary Conditions
*   **Boundary Conditions (B.C.):** Conditions at the ends of the domain (e.g., $x=0$ and $x=1$).
*   **Initial Conditions (I.C.):** Initial displacement $\theta(x)$ and initial velocity $\psi(x)$ at $t=0$.

### Solution Algorithm (7 Steps)
1.  **Homogenization**: If boundary conditions are non-homogeneous, they are reduced to zeros using a linear function $s(x,t) = (1-x)g_1(t) + xg_2(t)$.
2.  **Transformation**: Recalculating the source term $\tilde{f}(x,t)$ and initial conditions $\tilde{\theta}(x)$, $\tilde{\psi}(x)$ for the new function $v(x,t)$.
3.  **Eigenvalue Problem**: Solving the spatial problem to find $\lambda_k = k\pi$ and $X_k(x) = \sin(k\pi x)$.
4.  **Fourier Expansion**: Expanding the initial physical parameters into Fourier series relative to the eigenfunctions.
5.  **Time Domain ODE**: Solving the second-order ODE: $T_k''(t) + \lambda_k^2 T_k(t) = \mu_k(t)$. This step differs from the parabolic case as it accounts for oscillation rather than decay.
6.  **Synthesis**: Constructing $v(x,t) = \sum T_k(t)X_k(x)$.
7.  **Final Result**: Combining $u(x,t) = v(x,t) + s(x,t)$.
