# Lab 2.2: Mathematical Modeling (Projectile Motion & Spring-Mass System)

## Work Description
This laboratory work focuses on building and analyzing mathematical models for two classic physical systems:
1. **Projectile Motion with Air Resistance**: Studying how linear and quadratic drag forces affect the trajectory, range, and time of flight of an object thrown at an angle.
2. **Damped Harmonic Oscillator (Spring-Mass System)**: Modeling the oscillations of a mass Attached to a spring, considering both ideal (no damping) and real-world (linear damping) scenarios.

The goal is to derive the governing differential equations, solve them numerically using the Runge-Kutta method (RK45), and compare the results with analytical solutions where possible.

## Mathematical Models

### 1. Projectile Motion
The motion is described by a system of second-order ODEs (split into first-order):
- **Linear Resistance**: $F_{drag} \propto v$
  $$\frac{dv_x}{dt} = -\alpha v_x, \quad \frac{dv_y}{dt} = -g - \alpha v_y$$
- **Quadratic Resistance**: $F_{drag} \propto v^2$
  $$\frac{dv_x}{dt} = -\frac{C}{m} v \cdot v_x, \quad \frac{dv_y}{dt} = -g - \frac{C}{m} v \cdot v_y$$
  where $v = \sqrt{v_x^2 + v_y^2}$.

### 2. Spring-Mass System
The system follows Newton's Second Law: $m\ddot{x} + \alpha\dot{x} + kx = 0$.
- **No Damping ($\alpha=0$)**: Pure harmonic oscillations $x(t) = A\cos(\omega_0 t + \phi)$.
- **Linear Damping**: Damped oscillations $x(t) = e^{-\gamma t}(A\cos(\omega_d t) + B\sin(\omega_d t))$.

## Implementation & Results
- **Projectile**: Numerical results show that quadratic resistance significantly reduces the flight range and maximum height compared to the linear model and the vacuum (no resistance) case. The analytical solution for the linear case perfectly matches the numerical RK45 output.
- **Spring**: The model demonstrates energy dissipation over time in the damped case. The amplitude decreases exponentially, which is visualized through phase portraits showing a spiral trajectory toward the equilibrium point (stable focus).
